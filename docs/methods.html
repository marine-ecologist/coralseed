Methods

2023-10-03

Particle models

coralseed can be run with any dispersal model input with spatial points in an (x, y, time) format. The current version of coralseed was parameterised using a high-resolution advection/diffusion model CONNIE (Condie et al. 2012), which provides x,y points for each particle with 12 minute time-steps.

coralseed model

coralseed is parameterised from three main components: 1) Individual-based probability of competency, 2) probability of mortality through time, and 3) probability of settlement given a suitable habitat. From a probabilistic perspective, the model determines for each individual the conditional probability P(C) that an individual coral larvae will become competent, avoid mortality, and settle if they intercept a suitable habitat. The model is written in R using the sf (Pebesma & Bivand 2023) and datatable (Dowle & Srinivasan 2023) packages, with brms used for modelling response variables (Bürkner 2018).

1: Individual-based probability of competency

coralseed is flexibly parameterised to accept any input competency curves in the form of a list of posterior draws from Bayesian models of larval competency. To obtain the base parameterisation, a multi-level bayesian survival model was fit to the experimental data in brms :

```[r}

brm(minutes | cens(1 - settled) ~ (1 | w | tile),

```

Let P competency be the probability that an individual transitions from incompetent to competent.

\(P(T) = 1 - \exp\left(-\left(\frac{t}{\lambda}\right)^k\right)\)

where

  • t: time at which the larvae become competent
  • λ: scale parameter of the Weibull distribution
  • k: shape parameter of the Weibull distribution

2: Probablity of mortality through time

Let P mortality be the probability that an individual dies over a 24hr period.

\(P(M) = 1 - \exp\left(-\left(\frac{t}{\lambda_m}\right)^{k_m}\right)\)

where

  • t: time over a 24hr period
  • λm​: fixed scale parameter for the Weibull distribution for mortality
  • km​: fixed shape parameter for the Weibull distribution for mortality

3: Probability of settlement in a given habitat

Let P settlement be the probability of an individual settling following a Bernoulli distribution for P within a given habitat where

\(P(S|T, \neg M) = p_h\)

is the probability of settlement given that the individual is competent and has not experienced mortality.”

Combined probability

Thus, combined conditional probability P(C) hat an individual will become competent, stay alive, and settle if they pass over a suitable habitat is:

P(C)=P(T)×(1−P(M))×P(S∣T,¬M)P(C)=P(T)×(1−P(M))×P(S∣T,¬M)
